## Module 1 (Weeks 1–4): Guidelines for Final Year Project Proposal Development

The proposal stage is a crucial step in your final year project. It serves as a blueprint that defines your research focus, methodology, and expected outcomes.

### Identification of a Researchable Topic

- **Align with Your Field of Study:** Your topic must be relevant to Computer Science, Cybersecurity, Data Science, Information Technology, or Software Engineering.
- **Identify a Real-World Problem:** Look for challenges in industry, academia, or society that can be addressed through computing solutions. Some are available on uPivotal.
- **Leverage Emerging Trends:** Consider topics in AI, blockchain, cloud computing, IoT, cybersecurity threats, big data analytics, or software optimisation.
- **Feasibility Check:** Ensure that the topic is achievable within the available time, resources, and expertise.

> **Tip:** Discuss potential topics with your supervisor to refine your ideas.

### Literature Review to Identify Research Gap

- **Search for Relevant Papers:** Use Google Scholar, IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, and research repositories to find peer-reviewed articles.
- **Summarise Key Findings:** Identify previous work done on similar topics, highlighting methodologies and results.
- **Identify the Research Gap:** Determine what is missing in existing studies—whether it's a new approach, a limitation, or an unexplored area.
- **Validate the Problem Statement:** Use your findings to justify the need for your research.

> **Tip:** Aim for at least 20–30 recent and quality references from credible sources. Main Literature Review in your Chapter 2 must have at least 50 recent and quality references.

### Design and Development of Research Methodology

- **Research Approach:** Decide if your project is experimental, analytical, qualitative, or quantitative.
- **Data Collection (if applicable):** Specify how data will be gathered—surveys, web scraping, IoT sensors, APIs, etc.
- **Tools and Technologies:** List programming languages, frameworks, cloud services, or datasets you will use.
- **Implementation Plan:** Provide an overview of how you will develop your system, software, or model.

> **Tip:** Make your methodology specific and replicable for future researchers. Something that must be reproducible!

### Drafting and Submission of a Detailed Proposal

Your proposal should follow this structure:

#### Title Page

- Project title (concise and descriptive)
- Student's name and matriculation number
- Supervisor's name
- Department and institution

#### Introduction

- Brief background on the research topic
- Importance and motivation for the study
- Overview of the research problem

#### Problem Statement

- Clearly define the problem you aim to solve
- Justify why it is significant and needs a solution

#### Objectives

- Outline 3–5 clear, achievable goals of your study
- Use action verbs like "develop", "analyse", "evaluate", or "design"

#### Literature Review

- Summarize related works, highlighting gaps and opportunities

#### Methodology

- Explain your research approach, tools, and expected results

#### Expected Contributions

- Describe how your project will advance knowledge, improve processes, or solve a problem

#### Timeline & Resources

- Provide a project schedule with key milestones
- List software, hardware, and data requirements

#### References

- Cite all sources in **APA format**

> **Tip:** Keep your proposal concise (10–15 pages) and well-formatted for readability. Your main Project Documentation will be longer in Project II.

### Final Submission

- Proofread your document for errors.
- Submit on time according to the department's deadline.
- Prepare for feedback and be ready to refine your proposal and begin your Project.

## Module 2 (Weeks 5–8): Preliminary Research, Planning, and Presentation

After developing a solid project proposal, the next phase involves detailed planning, initial research, and presenting your findings for feedback.

### Development of a Project Plan

- **Break Down the Project into Phases:** Identify key stages such as research, design, implementation, testing, and report writing.
- **Create a Timeline:** Use Gantt charts or project management tools (e.g., uPivotal – this is compulsory for all learners) to outline tasks and deadlines.
- **Resource Allocation:** Identify software, hardware, datasets, and human resources needed for your project.
- **Risk Assessment:** Consider potential challenges (e.g., data availability, software bugs) and mitigation strategies.

> **Tip:** Set realistic deadlines and buffer periods to accommodate unforeseen challenges.

### Initial Data Collection and Analysis (if applicable)

- **Identify Data Sources:** Determine whether you'll collect primary data (e.g., surveys, experiments) or use secondary data (e.g., datasets from Kaggle, APIs, government portals).
- **Data Preprocessing:** Clean and structure your data (handling missing values, removing duplicates, etc.).
- **Preliminary Analysis:** Perform exploratory data analysis (EDA) to identify patterns, trends, or anomalies.
- **Selection of Analytical Tools:** Choose appropriate tools (Python, R, Power BI, Tableau, SQL) based on your data type and research objectives.

> **Tip:** If your project does not involve data collection, focus on setting up a prototype, framework, or system architecture.

### Preparation of Interim Reports

An interim report provides an update on your progress.

#### Interim Report Structure

- **Title Page:** Project title, student details, supervisor's name
- **Introduction:** Brief summary of the project and objectives
- **Literature Review Summary:** Key insights from existing research
- **Methodology Overview:** Outline of tools, technologies, and methods used
- **Preliminary Findings:** Summary of early results (data analysis, prototype development)
- **Challenges and Adjustments:** Problems faced and how you plan to resolve them
- **Updated Project Plan:** Revised timeline and next steps

> **Tip:** Include visuals (charts, tables) where necessary. Meet your supervisor often; you may submit your work to him/her chapter by chapter and not necessarily as outlined in the Interim Report Structure above.

## Module 3 (Weeks 9–12): Presentation and Feedback

### Oral Presentation of Proposal and Preliminary Findings

You will present your project proposal and early progress to a panel of faculty members.

#### Presentation Structure

| Slide | Content |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | Title, name, matriculation number, supervisor's name |
| 2 | Introduction (background, problem statement, objectives) |
| 3–4 | Literature review highlights (research gap) |
| 5–6 | Methodology (tools, data sources, system architecture) |
| 7 | Preliminary results (if applicable) |
| 8 | Challenges and solutions |
| 9 | Timeline and next steps |
| 10 | Conclusion and acknowledgments |

> **Tip:** Keep slides clear and minimal—use diagrams, tables, and bullet points instead of long paragraphs.
